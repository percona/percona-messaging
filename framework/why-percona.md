# Why Percona

Percona's approach is built on three core values that guide how we work and why clients choose us: our open source commitment, vendor freedom to run anywhere, and expertise on call.

### 1. Open alternatives commitment (free software, no vendor lock-in)

Since 2006, Percona has focused on maintaining and supporting open source database software that emphasizes user freedom, transparency, and portability. When certain upstream vendors adopt restrictive licenses, Percona maintains open alternatives, built to preserve compatibility, enterprise readiness, and the ability to run anywhere. This open approach informs our business practices, demonstrated by clear support lifecycles, transparent pricing, and a long-term participation in upstream communities.

- **Open alternatives with enterprise operational tooling:** Percona publishes open MySQL and PostgreSQL distributions, supports **MariaDB (Community)** in line with [Percona's MariaDB support scope](https://www.percona.com/services/support/mariadb-support), maintains a source-available MongoDB build, supports users of community-led projects like Valkey, and provides open source Kubernetes Operators for MySQL, PostgreSQL, and MongoDB as alternatives to vendor-tied orchestration platforms. These distributions and operators include the components needed for production environments, implemented with the appropriate tools for each engine, while remaining fully inspectable and free of proprietary licensing.
- **Predictable costs through open licensing:** Percona's database software is free, helping teams avoid recurring subscription fees and maintain stable, usage-based operating costs.
- **Governance clarity:** Database ecosystems differ in ownership. MySQL and MongoDB are vendor-governed, PostgreSQL is community-governed, and Valkey is community-led under the Linux Foundation. These differences influence roadmap control, licensing exposure, and long-term cloud strategy. Percona's independence, with freedom from VC ownership and no licensing agenda, gives customers one stable, neutral partner across ecosystems and models, reducing governance-driven risk.
- **Deep Ecosystem participation:** Percona engineers actively contribute across multiple open source database ecosystems. We maintain transparent engineering processes and community-driven development across all our products, allowing users and upstream projects to benefit from our work.
  - **PostgreSQL ecosystem:** We have introduced SMGR and WAL hook APIs in Percona Server for PostgreSQL and developed an OIDC validator, enabling secure deployments and broader extension support. We package [pgBackRest](https://pgbackrest.org/) in Percona Distribution for PostgreSQL, release-test it with Patroni and adjacent operational components, and are a [coalition sponsor of ongoing pgBackRest development](https://pgbackrest.org/news.html#will-continue) so customers keep an inspectable, PiTR-capable PostgreSQL backup baseline on release-tested distribution binaries instead of assembling tooling alone.
  - **MySQL ecosystem:** We maintain open operational tooling such as [Percona XtraBackup](https://docs.percona.com/percona-xtrabackup/8.4/) and [Percona XtraDB Cluster](https://docs.percona.com/percona-xtradb-cluster/8.4/), including our own open Galera fork so Percona XtraDB Cluster customers keep the same HA stack without replatforming when MySQL Galera Cluster reaches end of life, with continued Percona releases and long-term support on the same lifecycle terms as today, and we accept and integrate community contributions through the Percona Operator for MySQL. In February 2026, Percona published an [open letter](https://letter.3306-db.org/) with other MySQL community members, inviting Oracle to discuss vendor-neutral foundation models for shared ecosystem work (for example public bug tracking and community programs).
  - **MongoDB ecosystem:** We build and maintain a source-available MongoDB-compatible server, along with open tooling such as [Percona Backup for MongoDB](https://docs.percona.com/percona-backup-mongodb/index.html) and [Percona ClusterSync for MongoDB](https://docs.percona.com/percona-clustersync-for-mongodb/index.html), enabling backup, data movement, and replication workflows compatible with upstream MongoDB.
  - **Valkey ecosystem:** We publish distribution packages and migration tooling, aligned with the community-led roadmap under the Linux Foundation.
- **Continuity through change:** When upstream licensing shifts, Percona sustains compatible, open alternatives so customers avoid abrupt lock-in or unsupported transitions.
- **Independent by design:** As a fully bootstrapped and independent company, Percona gives customers long-term stability with no risk of VC-driven licensing changes or vendor lock-in surprises.
- **Lifecycle clarity:** Each Percona database product follows a [defined lifecycle](https://www.percona.com/services/policies/percona-software-support-lifecycle) that governs how long new builds, backports, and fixes are provided. While we align closely with upstream timelines, we sometimes extend support or offer **[End of Life (EOL) programs](https://www.percona.com/services/policies/percona-software-support-lifecycle)** to give customers more flexibility and stability during transitions. For release-timing communication, Percona aligns customer-facing statements to published lifecycle and release-note sources rather than unconfirmed internal delivery estimates.

### 2. Run anywhere (Multi-cloud / Multi-platform freedom)

Deployments work across on-premises, hybrid, or cloud platforms with predictable support coverage for reduced vendor lock-in risk. Customers choose based on cost, compliance, or performance, not on vendor restrictions. Percona ensures customers stay in control of their environments, budgets, and compliance posture as they move between platforms.

- **Support and portability across major clouds and operating systems:** Percona supports deployments on AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft Azure, Alibaba, and across operating systems including Debian, Ubuntu LTS, Amazon Linux, Red Hat Enterprise Linux (RHEL), and RHEL derivatives such as Rocky Linux and AlmaLinux. [LeadByte migrated from AWS Aurora to a self-managed, two-datacenter Percona XtraDB Cluster](https://www.percona.com/about-percona/case-studies/leadbyte-migrates-from-aws-to-percona), gaining higher availability and performance while eliminating cloud licensing premiums and regaining full control over their architecture.
- **Configuration is portable** through declarative YAML manifests and Kubernetes-native APIs, ensuring consistency across environments and clouds. Percona Operators are compatible with Operator Lifecycle Manager (OLM) and integrate with Percona Monitoring and Management (PMM) for enterprise-grade automation and observability. [Carpages.ca](https://www.percona.com/about-percona/case-studies/carpagesca) unified Percona Server for MySQL, XtraDB Cluster, ProxySQL, the Kubernetes Operator, and PMM to achieve zero-downtime migrations, 60–100% traffic growth handling, and continuous uptime across Kubernetes environments, exemplifying enterprise-grade open source portability in production. This lets teams standardize operations and policy enforcement across environments and avoid being trapped by any single cloud vendor.
- **Percona Operators** make MySQL, PostgreSQL, and MongoDB compatible with CI/CD pipelines, GitOps workflows, and Kubernetes environments. Backup and restore processes rely on open tooling matched to each engine, such as XtraBackup for MySQL-compatible clusters and pgBackRest, pg_dump, or barman for PostgreSQL, ensuring data mobility across clouds and clusters without vendor lock-in.
- **Percona Monitoring and Management (PMM)** delivers unified observability across hybrid and multi-cloud deployments.

### 3. Expertise on call (24x7 access to upstream-level engineers)

Percona's core differentiator is its people: engineers who contribute to and deeply understand the upstream code for MySQL, MariaDB, PostgreSQL, MongoDB, Valkey, and Redis. These are the specialists customers call when issues exceed what cloud vendors, DBaaS platforms, or typical consultancies can resolve. Across Support, ExpertOps, and Consulting, customers work directly with experts to solve edge cases, design the hard architectural work, and keep critical systems reliable.

- **24×7×365 and clear SLAs:** 30 minutes for Severity-1 on Advanced Support and 15 minutes on Premium Support. Whether reactive during incidents or proactive through ExpertOps, Percona experts engage quickly and with deep technical accuracy across all supported technologies.
- **Optional personalized support:** For customers who need closer partnership, Percona offers named Service Delivery Managers, embedded communication channels (including dedicated Slack and ticket portals), and regular operational reviews when included in the agreement. Experts aren't just available, we're actively present.
- **Prepared for escalation:** Incident management follows a defined escalation path. Root cause analysis is not part of the standard Expert Support SLA. Proactive, hands-on management is delivered separately through ExpertOps, and complex project work is handled through Expert Consulting and Services.
