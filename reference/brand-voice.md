# Brand Voice and Style Guide

This is the tool-agnostic reference for Percona's brand voice, tone, and style rules. The same rules are encoded in `.cursor/rules/brand-voice.mdc` for AI editor users.

## Channel defaults

| Channel | Voice |
| --- | --- |
| Website landing pages + PDFs | Challenger voice: confident, plainspoken, slightly rebellious |
| Technical docs / runbooks | Neutral technical: direct, minimal tone |
| Blogs / solution briefs | Challenger-lite: neutral body, sharper headings |
| Legal / compliance pages | Strictly neutral |

## Challenger voice rules

- **Headings**: bold, short, concrete. Brand personality lives here.
- **Body**: calm, factual, skimmable. Short paragraphs, bullets, defined acronyms.
- **Snark**: allowed in headings and callouts, must be fact-anchored. Aim at constraints and outcomes, not people.
- **"Freedom"**: preferred brand word in headings. Means portability, control, optionality. Anchor to a concrete "how" within 1-2 lines. One strong use per page max.

## Words and phrases to avoid

**AI-sounding jargon**: leverage, unlock, empower, enable, drive outcomes, robust, seamless, scalable, future-proof, best-in-class, delve into, in today's landscape, at scale (when vague), streamline, optimize, modernize (without mechanism)

**Marketing hype**: game-changing, revolutionary, cutting-edge, innovative, world-class, industry-leading, next-generation, synergy, value-add, transformative, holistic

**Military language**: battle-tested, war room, arsenal, weapon, kill, crush, dominate, campaign, tactics

**Ideology-first framing** (in body copy): "we believe," "our mission," "revolution"

## Words and phrases to prefer

Concrete verbs: builds, maintains, supports, contributes, publishes, configures, tunes, monitors

Plain problem/outcome language: "reduce downtime," "avoid vendor lock-in," "control your data layer"

## Naming hierarchy

Use the most defensible formulation. In order of preference:

1. **Percona leads** (strongest): "Percona Software for MongoDB," "Percona Support for MySQL"
2. **"for [DB]"** (defensible): "Support for MongoDB," "Expert Support for PostgreSQL"
3. **"[DB] [Service]"** (weakest, avoid in headlines): "MongoDB Support," "MySQL Consulting"

## Trademark rules

- Use ® on first or prominent mention of registered marks (MongoDB®, MySQL®)
- Never pair a trademark with copy that disparages the trademark owner in the same headline, subhead, or CTA
- See NOTICES.md for full trademark attribution

## Review checklist

Before finalizing any marketing asset:
- [ ] No headline/CTA pairs a trademark with disparagement
- [ ] Service headers use "Percona [X] for [DB]" or "[X] for [DB]"
- [ ] ® on first/prominent mention of registered marks
- [ ] SSPL products called "source available," not "open source"
- [ ] No fabricated claims (stats attributed, proof points verified)
- [ ] Channel-appropriate tone
- [ ] No banned words (AI jargon, military metaphors, hype without mechanism)
- [ ] Short paragraphs, clear headings, defined acronyms
