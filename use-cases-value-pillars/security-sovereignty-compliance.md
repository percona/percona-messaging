# Security, Sovereignty, and Compliance

Freedom in data operations depends on trust: the ability to know where your data lives, how it's protected, and whether it meets regulatory and business standards anywhere you operate. No longer just boxes to tick, security and compliance can limit how freely an enterprise can operate.

Across industries, governance, compliance, and hardening requirements are expanding, from GDPR, HIPAA, and PCI-DSS to DISA's STIG guidelines used in public-sector and defense environments. At the same time, hyperscaler ecosystems make it difficult for enterprises to verify what "secure" really means inside managed services. [UNCTAD's overview of data protection and privacy legislation worldwide](https://unctad.org/page/data-protection-and-privacy-legislation-worldwide) documents how many jurisdictions have adopted formal regimes, which enterprises increasingly pair with sovereign-cloud or regional-control strategies. [IDC's Cloud Pulse 2Q22](https://www.bigdatawire.com/this-just-in/idc-survey-finds-data-sovereignty-and-compliance-issues-shaping-it-decisions/) reports that nearly half of enterprises now cite data sovereignty and compliance as top design priorities for future architectures.

Percona helps enterprises stay secure, compliant, and operationally free on their own terms, not their vendor's.

### The Problem: You Can't Verify or Control Security Inside Managed Services

Enterprises are expected to demonstrate airtight security and regulatory compliance while their infrastructure grows more fragmented across clouds and vendors. As multi-cloud adoption expands, regulators are increasingly demanding demonstrable control, not just contractual assurances. Yet most proprietary database services make it impossible to inspect, verify, or standardize controls, leaving organizations exposed to both risk and dependency.

- **Opaque compliance frameworks:** Vendor certifications often mask how encryption, replication, and patching are implemented. Customers can't independently verify their environments end to end, leading to uncertainty during audits.
- **Lock-in through tooling:** Vendor-specific APIs and managed encryption layers limit portability and make it costly to adapt to new regulatory or business needs. Many firms cite vendor dependency and lack of portability as strategic risks. Adopters of sovereign or independent infrastructure cite freedom from vendor lock-in and stronger strategic control as key benefits ([PureStorage](https://www.purestorage.com/company/newsroom/press-releases/data-sovereignty-emerges-as-critical-business-risk-in-new-geopolitical-era-in.html)).
- **Fragmented control policies:** Security enforcement differs across platforms: one engine's encryption standard or retention policy rarely matches another's, creating audit gaps and inconsistent governance. One survey flagged legal and geopolitical dependencies (46%) and cybersecurity incidents (42%) as key drivers for IT sovereignty initiatives ([HMS](https://www.analytical-software.de/en/it-sovereignty-in-practice/)).
- **Unverified supply chains:** Only about 20% of organizations generate software bills of materials (SBOMs), leaving most without full traceability into the software they depend on ([arXiv, 2025](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2503.15021)).
- **Costly reactive posture:** Without unified visibility, teams discover vulnerabilities or compliance gaps after incidents, not before. Fines for data misuse and breaches are climbing, and the average cost of a breach reached $4.88M ([IBM](https://www.ibm.com/think/insights/cost-of-a-data-breach-2024-financial-industry)).

For CISOs and data leaders, the risk isn't noncompliance itself: it's losing the ability to prove compliance.

### The Solution: Transparent, Unified, and Free

Percona's approach is built on transparency and open source stewardship. By combining open source transparency with enterprise-grade security and lifecycle management, Percona helps organizations stay compliant, secure, and independent across any environment.

- **Sovereign control:** Percona gives organizations full visibility into their database environments and the freedom to run them on any infrastructure, whether public cloud, private cloud, hybrid, or air-gapped, without relying on proprietary operational layers. This keeps data location, governance, and enforcement under the organization's control rather than the vendor's.
- **Open and verifiable software:** We publish open source MySQL and PostgreSQL distributions, support **MariaDB (Community)**, maintain a source-available MongoDB build, and contribute upstream to projects like Valkey while supporting their users. Because the code is accessible, customers can verify important security features like encryption, access control, and runtime logic, and choose infrastructure components without relying solely on proprietary vendors.
- **Predictable governance:** Database ecosystems follow different governance models. MySQL and MongoDB are vendor-governed, PostgreSQL is community-governed, and Valkey is community-led under the Linux Foundation. Clear governance lets customers assess roadmap control, licensing exposure, and long-term cloud strategy.
- **Standardized encryption and access:** Percona's distributions implement transparent data-at-rest encryption across [PostgreSQL](https://docs.percona.com/postgresql/), [MySQL](https://www.percona.com/mysql/software), and [MongoDB](https://www.percona.com/mongodb/software) variants, along with WAL or redo-log encryption where applicable. All engines also integrate TLS and role-based access by default, supporting global compliance standards such as GDPR, HIPAA, and ISO 27001. On Kubernetes, declarative Custom Resources carry the same discipline into policy, credentials, and topology: see [Percona for MongoDB multi-cluster and multi-region deployments](https://docs.percona.com/percona-operator-for-mongodb/replication.html), [MySQL Operator Custom Resource options](https://docs.percona.com/percona-operator-for-mysql/pxc/operator.html), and [Percona for MongoDB Operator Vault integration](https://docs.percona.com/percona-operator-for-mongodb/system-users-vault.html).
- **Transparent vulnerability management:** Every Percona product follows a [documented CVE process](https://www.percona.com/security) from disclosure to patch release, giving customers visibility into resolution timelines and applied fixes. Percona publishes CVE advisories publicly, allowing customers to validate remediation timelines and patch alignment. Alongside that patch discipline, PMM Advisors surface Security, Configuration, Performance, and Query findings where teams already run observability, so common misconfigurations and database-layer risks surface before they escalate; advisor results stay on PMM Server and are not sent as telemetry ([PMM Advisor documentation](https://docs.percona.com/percona-monitoring-and-management/3/advisors/advisors.html)). Supported PMM releases also mask common database credentials in diagnostic logs, lowering accidental leakage risk when teams share PMM output during escalations ([credential masking in logs](https://docs.percona.com/percona-monitoring-and-management/3/release-notes/3.7.1.html#safer-log-sharing-with-automatic-credential-masking)).
- **Operational resilience with Experts:** Percona's 24×7 Expert teams validate backups, run live recovery drills, and maintain RTOs under 15 minutes. By augmenting internal staff and filling critical operational skill gaps, Percona ensures resilience and regulatory readiness, especially valuable for teams facing internal staffing or skill constraints.
