# Security, Sovereignty, and Compliance

Freedom in data operations depends on trust: the ability to know where your data lives, how it's protected, and whether it meets regulatory and business standards anywhere you operate. No longer just boxes to tick, security and compliance can limit how freely an enterprise can operate.

Sovereignty is not only where data sits. It is who can operate your databases and whether you can keep running under supplier or jurisdictional change. Percona delivers software, Expert Support, and Expert Consulting and Services that help teams exercise **data**, **operational**, and **technological** sovereignty across MySQL, MariaDB (Community), PostgreSQL, MongoDB-compatible, Valkey, and Redis. The primary risk is **concentration and continuity risk**: single-provider dependency when teams cannot prove residency, access control, or exit paths.

Across industries, governance, compliance, and hardening requirements are expanding, from GDPR, HIPAA, and PCI-DSS to DISA's STIG guidelines used in public-sector and defense environments. At the same time, hyperscaler ecosystems make it difficult for enterprises to verify what "secure" really means inside managed services. [UNCTAD's overview of data protection and privacy legislation worldwide](https://unctad.org/page/data-protection-and-privacy-legislation-worldwide) documents how many jurisdictions have adopted formal regimes, which enterprises increasingly pair with sovereign-cloud or regional-control strategies. National frameworks beyond Europe, including India's DPDP Act and Brazil's LGPD, add distinct residency and access rules ([Forcepoint global data protection laws, 2026](https://www.forcepoint.com/blog/insights/tracking-global-data-protection-laws-2026)). A majority of organizations prioritize data sovereignty in infrastructure decisions, with strong regional weight in Europe, Middle East, and APAC ([Thales Global Data Threat Report](https://cpl.thalesgroup.com/data-threat-report)).

Percona is a portable, supported, self-managed database stack for teams that must prove control of data and operations on-premises, in private cloud, and on Kubernetes, with open source transparency and Expert Support instead of opaque DBaaS lock-in or uncoordinated DIY risk.

### The Problem: You Can't Verify or Control Security Inside Managed Services

Enterprises are expected to demonstrate airtight security and regulatory compliance while their infrastructure grows more fragmented across clouds and vendors. As multi-cloud adoption expands, regulators are increasingly demanding demonstrable control, not just contractual assurances. Yet most proprietary database services make it impossible to inspect, verify, or standardize controls, leaving organizations exposed to both risk and dependency.

- **Opaque compliance frameworks:** Vendor certifications often mask how encryption, replication, and patching are implemented. Customers can't independently verify their environments end to end, leading to uncertainty during audits.
- **Lock-in through tooling:** Vendor-specific APIs and managed encryption layers limit portability and make it costly to adapt to new regulatory or business needs. Many firms cite vendor dependency and lack of portability as strategic risks. Adopters of sovereign or independent infrastructure cite freedom from vendor lock-in and stronger strategic control as key benefits ([PureStorage](https://www.purestorage.com/company/newsroom/press-releases/data-sovereignty-emerges-as-critical-business-risk-in-new-geopolitical-era-in.html)).
- **Fragmented control policies:** Security enforcement differs across platforms: one engine's encryption standard or retention policy rarely matches another's, creating audit gaps and inconsistent governance. One survey flagged legal and geopolitical dependencies (46%) and cybersecurity incidents (42%) as key drivers for IT sovereignty initiatives ([HMS](https://www.analytical-software.de/en/it-sovereignty-in-practice/)).
- **Unverified supply chains:** Only about 20% of organizations generate software bills of materials (SBOMs), leaving most without full traceability into the software they depend on ([arXiv, 2025](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2503.15021)).
- **Costly reactive posture:** Without unified visibility, teams discover vulnerabilities or compliance gaps after incidents, not before. Fines for data misuse and breaches are climbing, and the average cost of a breach reached $4.88M ([IBM](https://www.ibm.com/think/insights/cost-of-a-data-breach-2024-financial-industry)).

For CISOs and data leaders, the risk isn't noncompliance itself: it's losing the ability to prove compliance.

### The Solution: Transparent, Unified, and Free

Percona's approach is built on transparency and open source stewardship. By combining open source transparency with enterprise-grade security and lifecycle management, Percona helps organizations stay compliant, secure, and independent across any environment.

- **Predictable governance:** Database ecosystems follow different governance models. MySQL and MongoDB are vendor-governed, PostgreSQL is community-governed, and Valkey is community-led under the Linux Foundation. Clear governance lets customers assess roadmap control, licensing exposure, and long-term cloud strategy.
- **Standardized encryption and access:** Percona's distributions implement transparent data-at-rest encryption at the database layer where the engine supports it (for example pg_tde on PostgreSQL with 256-bit AES, WAL encryption, and pg_tde_upgrade for encrypted-cluster upgrades, rather than relying on storage-only disk encryption alone), across [PostgreSQL](https://docs.percona.com/postgresql/), [MySQL](https://www.percona.com/mysql/software), and [MongoDB](https://www.percona.com/mongodb/software) variants, along with WAL or redo-log encryption where applicable ([Percona Transparent Data Encryption for PostgreSQL](https://docs.percona.com/pg-tde/)). All engines also integrate TLS and role-based access by default, supporting global compliance standards such as GDPR, HIPAA, and ISO 27001. On Kubernetes, declarative Custom Resources carry the same discipline into policy, credentials, and topology: see [Percona for MongoDB multi-cluster and multi-region deployments](https://docs.percona.com/percona-operator-for-mongodb/replication.html), [MySQL Operator Custom Resource options](https://docs.percona.com/percona-operator-for-mysql/pxc/operator.html), and [Percona for MongoDB Operator Vault integration](https://docs.percona.com/percona-operator-for-mongodb/system-users-vault.html).
- **Safer diagnostic artifacts:** PMM masks common database credentials in diagnostic logs so teams can share PMM output during vendor or internal escalations with lower risk of accidental secret leakage ([credential masking in logs](https://docs.percona.com/percona-monitoring-and-management/3/release-notes/3.7.1.html#safer-log-sharing-with-automatic-credential-masking)).
- **Transparent vulnerability management:** Every Percona product follows a [documented CVE process](https://www.percona.com/security) from disclosure to patch release, giving customers visibility into resolution timelines and applied fixes. Percona publishes CVE advisories publicly, allowing customers to validate remediation timelines and patch alignment. Alongside that patch discipline, PMM Advisors surface Security, Configuration, Performance, and Query findings where teams already run observability, so common misconfigurations and database-layer risks surface before they escalate; advisor results stay on PMM Server and are not sent as telemetry ([PMM Advisor documentation](https://docs.percona.com/percona-monitoring-and-management/3/advisors/advisors.html)).
- **Operational resilience with Experts:** Percona's 24×7 Expert teams validate backups, run live recovery drills, and maintain RTOs under 15 minutes. By augmenting internal staff and filling critical operational skill gaps, Percona ensures resilience and regulatory readiness, especially valuable for teams facing internal staffing or skill constraints.

### Sovereignty framework: data, operational, and technological

Compliance at scale requires more than data residency. It requires operational control (who can touch systems) and technology continuity (how you keep running if a provider or jurisdiction changes). Percona maps buyer outcomes to three pillars and backs them with verifiable evidence.

| Pillar | What it means (buyer lens) | Percona controls | Verification evidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Data sovereignty** | You control **where** data resides and which laws apply. | Region pinning; on-prem, private, and sovereign cloud deployments; Operators with placement constraints; backup target controls; diagnostics and advisors without mandatory outbound telemetry. | Residency attestation (region map, backup target list); storage-class policy; no-mandatory-telemetry statement; network egress report. |
| **Operational sovereignty** | You control **who** can operate, access, and support systems. | Jurisdiction-scoped Expert Support options where offered; RBAC; SSO/OIDC; customer-managed keys via database or KMS integration; air-gapped operation where required; standard logs and metrics for ELK, Prometheus, or Grafana; PMM for access and change visibility. | Who-can-touch-what roster; S1 escalation policy by jurisdiction (subject to staffing and SLA); ticket data path; RBAC configuration; audit log samples; DR drill report. |
| **Technological sovereignty** | You can **continue** running and evolve without a single vendor. | Open source MySQL and PostgreSQL; **MariaDB (Community)** within published Expert Support scope; **source-available** MongoDB-compatible software; Valkey/Redis support; Kubernetes Operators; portable backup/restore; documented exit and upgrade paths. | Per-workload exit plan; restore-from-backup proof; version alignment matrix; dependency BOM without proprietary runtime lock-in. |

### Use cases

- **Audit-ready sovereignty evidence:** Auditors and regulators ask for proof of residency, access boundaries, and exit paths, not vendor attestations alone. Teams produce artifacts mapped in the sovereignty framework (region maps, RBAC configuration, restore-from-backup proof, DR drill reports) using inspectable distributions, PMM, and Operators.
- **EU AI Act and AI data governance:** From 2 August 2026, organizations operating high-risk AI systems in the EU must demonstrate traceability and audit evidence for training and inference data stores. Percona distributions provide inspectable audit logging, TLS, and RBAC; Operators support placement constraints for AI data residency on infrastructure the customer controls.
- **GDPR and global data residency:** Personal data must stay in approved jurisdictions with auditable access control under GDPR, India's DPDP Act, Brazil's LGPD, and similar national frameworks. Percona supports customer-hosted deployments in regional or private clouds under customer control, with TLS and encryption at rest across major distributions when configured in the customer's compliance program.
- **CLOUD Act-sensitive workloads:** Teams that cannot rely on US hyperscaler control planes need hosting they choose, including non-US infrastructure. Percona does not host customer data; customers run the stack on infrastructure that matches their jurisdictional requirements.
- **Multi-tenant platform isolation:** Shared platforms must separate tenants or business units without control-policy drift. Percona Operators support Kubernetes namespaces, network policies, and RBAC so isolation stays consistent across MySQL, PostgreSQL, and MongoDB-compatible workloads.
