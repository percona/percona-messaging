# Origin

Percona Messaging started from a simple thesis: an open source company should manage its messaging with open source practices too.

This repository is not only a place to store copy. It is an operating model for making messaging easier to find, easier to review, easier to update, and easier to trust.

## The problem this model addresses

- Messaging often gets scattered across multiple tools and surfaces.
- The canonical source and review path are not always obvious to contributors.
- Updates can require coordination across multiple people, deliverables, and subject areas.
- Weak source material creates downstream inconsistency, especially when teams adapt it through AI-assisted or manual workflows.

## What this repository set out to prove

- Messaging can be managed as durable shared infrastructure.
- Contributors can work from a visible source of truth rather than private interpretation.
- Review becomes more efficient when ownership and approval paths are explicit.
- Reusable source material makes downstream deliverables more consistent.

## Design principles

- Keep durable messaging in tracked markdown.
- Treat brand alignment as a system requirement, not a final copy-edit step.
- Maintain canonical naming and terminology so product/offering language stays consistent across teams and channels.
- Keep voice and positioning coherent across framework, offerings, products, and shared value narratives.
- Keep rough planning and internal-only notes out of canonical docs.
- Pair content with governance so contributors know how decisions are made.
- Start with real, bounded source material rather than trying to migrate everything at once.
- Optimize for reuse and adaptation, not only for publication.

## How other teams can adapt this model

1. Choose a narrow first scope.
2. Define who owns accuracy, process, and final approval.
3. Document what stays private and what becomes canonical.
4. Test the workflow on a real change before broadening scope.
5. Turn what you learn into contribution guidance and review rules.
